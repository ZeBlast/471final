About This Project

After Graduation is a single-page D3 experience built on the U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard. It moves from a national scatter of earnings against admissions and cost signals, to a pan and zoom map, to a comparison workspace where saved schools sit side by side on tuition, debt, and earnings horizons.

Runtime Data: Most recent institution and field of study extracts in data/college_scorecard_data/ (see README).

This page mirrors the editorial layout of the main visualization, same navigation, typography, and method panels, so documentation feels like part of the story rather than a separate microsite.

README: Datasets, Columns, and How Each Section Consumes Them

The live site loads two Scorecard CSVs in the browser with D3. The repository also keeps optional merge scripts and legacy tables (for example PSEO joins) under data/. Those files are not required to run index.html.

Sections How the Visuals Use the Rows Above

Learn

Scatter of median earnings (one or four years after completion) versus admission rate, completer debt, or in state or out of state tuition. Filters by control segment, a “prestigious” private nonprofit rule of thumb, and state. This automatically generated trend notes under the chart.

Explore

Albers USA map with one point per institution that has coordinates. These include color and size encode earnings, earnings to debt ratio, debt, tuition, or Pell rate. Pell rate had state drill down, zoom, tooltips, and click to stage for Compare.

Compare

Search the full institution list, keep a tray of saved schools, toggle in state versus out of state tuition, and open per school panels with summary tiles plus a line chart of the two earnings horizons against completer debt and published tuition.

Source: U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard, download center at collegescorecard.ed.gov/data. Replace the CSVs in data/college_scorecard_data/ when the department publishes refreshed extracts. Transparency: AI Tool Usage

We used Anthropic Claude and Cursor to assist with the coding process, suggesting snippets, explaining errors, and speeding up refactors. None of that replaced the team’s work: every feature was implemented, reviewed, and owned in this repository, with final logic and integration written and tested by us.

Code: Claude in Cursor proposed starter D3 patterns for the scatter, map, and Compare line char. We then adapted them to the real Scorecard columns, accessibility attributes, and our interaction model.

Debugging: The same tools helped reason through filter and projection edge cases (empty states, suppressed values, resize). Fixes were applied and verified locally.

Data: Optional Python merge scaffolding was discussed in chat. The column names and joins were validated manually against CSV headers.

Writing and UX: Draft copy and layout ideas were iterated with Claude, then edited for accuracy, tone, and consistency with the rest of the site.
