About This Project

After Graduation is a single page site we built with D3 on top of the U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard. It is meant for anyone who wants a guided way to see how campuses compare on cost, borrowing, and pay after college before they dig into spreadsheets on their own.

We built this experience so the same public Scorecard numbers sit behind three simple moves on one page. You can start with a national scatter of campuses, open a map to browse by place, then save a handful of schools into a comparison view. The layout keeps attention on earnings, tuition, debt, and aid signals the Scorecard already publishes, instead of asking you to jump between tools.

The College Scorecard is a federal transparency program that publishes comparable figures on colleges and universities: typical costs, borrowing, graduation where available, and median earnings for students after they leave school. The goal is to make it easier for students and families to weigh price, aid, and long run outcomes alongside selectivity and geography.

On This Site: Learn, Explore, and Compare

Learn

Scatter of median earnings (one or four years after completion) versus admission rate, completer debt, or in state or out of state tuition. Filters by sector, a selective highlight for certain private nonprofit campuses, and state. Automatic trend notes summarize the filtered view above the chart.

Explore

United States map with one point per institution that reports coordinates. Color and size can show earnings, earnings to debt ratio, debt, tuition, or Pell rate. State drill down, zoom, tooltips, and click to stage campuses for Compare. The detail card can also surface program level earnings from the Scorecard where those fields exist.

Compare

Search the full institution list, keep a tray of saved schools, toggle in state versus out of state tuition, and open per school panels with summary tiles plus a line chart of the two earnings horizons against completer debt and published tuition.
