What do graduates earn after college?

The U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard publishes median earnings for students after they leave college, which can be an indicator for whether a degree pays off. This webpage starts with those earnings plotted against admissions, debt, and tuition so you can see where institutions cluster and who sits above or below the pattern. From there, the same measures are mapped across the country, then narrowed again so you can pull a short list of schools into a side-by-side comparison.

Source: U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard (latest college-level figures).

Median earnings are after completion only, one year and four years out, the same horizons as on the map. Debt is median borrowing for students who finished, not including parent loans. Tuition is published in-state or out-of-state sticker price; admission rate is the share of applicants admitted.

How to read this chart

Each dot is one campus. Adjust who appears, which state you include, what sits on the horizontal axis, and whether the vertical axis is pay one year or four years after completion. Takeaways that react to your filters appear under What stands out just above the plot.
